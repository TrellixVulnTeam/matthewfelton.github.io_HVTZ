Athens Alabama

Located in north-central Alabama, 15 miles from the Tennessee border, Athens is the county seat of Limestone County. Since its founding in 1818, one year before Alabama's admittance into the Union, Athens has contributed significantly to shaping the history of the state. Home to three Alabama governors and the state's oldest institution of higher education, Athens boasts a number of historic buildings and residences. Originally an agricultural community, Athens has evolved into a major center of technology in North Alabama. Athens has a mayor-council form of government.

Demographics

The population of Athens, according to 2016 Census estimates, was 24,461. Of that total, 74.4 percent of respondents identified themselves as white, 17.5 percent as African American, 8.8 percent Hispanic, 3.1 percent as two or more races, 0.9 percent as Native American, 0.7 percent as Asian, and 0.1 percent as Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander. The city's median household income was $44,920, and the per capita income was $28,865.

Education

The Athens City School System has four elementary schools, one intermediate school, one middle school, and one high school. The city has two private K-12 schools.
